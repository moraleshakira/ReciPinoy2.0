chicken pastil

Pastil, or pastel, is a Filipino packed rice meal made of steamed rice wrapped in banana leaves with dry shredded beef, chicken, or fish. The meat is prepared by browning it in vegetable oil with onion, garlic, and soy sauce.This dish is a Mindanao classic, also known as Patil, Patel, Patir, or pater in Maranao, and Paster in Iranun.

chicken pastil recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1/2 cup Canola Oil
  2. 2 cloves Garlic minced
  3. 1 med. Red Onion minced
  4. 350 g Chicken boiled & Shredded
  5. 4 tbsp Soy Sauce3 tbsp White Vinegar
  6. 11/2 tbsp White/Brown Sugar
  7. 1/2 tsp Black Pepper
  8. 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  9. 2 tbsp Margarine
  10. Banana leaves for wrapping

Instruction

    Shredded chicken

  1. In a pot add 1 liter of water, 1 tsp Salt and 1 tsp Black Pepper & 2 Laurel Leaves.
  2. Add 350g Chicken Breast Fillet.
  3. Boil on med. heat for about 15 mins.
  4. Drain, let it cool down & shred the chicken

    5. Let's fry

  5. In a fry pan pour Oil.
  6. Add minced Garlic and minced Red Onion.
  7. Fry until translucent then add 350g shredded chicken and saute.
  8. Add Black Pepper, Turmeric & Sugar.
  9. Add soy sauce & saute.
  10. Add vinegar & let it simmer.
  11. add margarine.
  12. Fry until chicken flakes are crispy.

    13. Assembly

  13. Originally Pastil is wrapped in Banana Leaves.
  14. cup hot rice in a plate then toppings it with the shredded chicken
  15. that's it. enjoy your pater!

chicken biryani

Biryani is a dish produced by the Muslim people in the Indian/Persian region. It's no surprise that this delectable delicacy has found its way into the Muslim community as part of Mindanao cuisine. Biryani is essentially a fried rice dish flavored with chicken or seafood and other spices.

fried chicken biryani recipe

Ingredients

  1. 3 cups basmati rice
  2. 1 whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  3. vegetable oil
  4. 2 potatoes
  5. 1 large carrot
  6. 2 tsp salt
  7. 2 pc onion
  8. 2 cloves garlic
  9. 2 ripe tomatoes
  10. 2 tbsp tomato paste
  11. 2 tbsp crushed fresh ginger root
  12. 2 tbsp green curry paste
  13. 1 tsp ground turmeric
  14. 1 tsp ground black pepper
  15. 1 cube chicken bouillon
  16. 1 tbsp lime juice

Instruction

  1. Place rice in a large bowl and cover with water. Let soak for 20 minutes; drain.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil; add 1 tablespoon oil and 1 teaspoon salt. Add rice; simmer until partially cooked through and still firm, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat 1 cup oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add potatoes and carrot; saut√© until golden, about 2 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to transfer vegetables to a plate. Add chicken pieces to remaining hot oil in the pot; fry until golden brown, about 5 minutes per side.Stir onions, tomatoes, tomato paste, ginger, curry paste, soy sauce, garlic, turmeric, remaining 1 teaspoon salt, black pepper, and chicken bouillon into chicken in the pot. Cover and simmer until flavors combine, about 5 minutes. Stir in lime juice. Return potatoes and carrot to the pot. Cook and stir for 1 minute. Remove from heat. Add hearts of palm (ubod). Cook for 5 minutes. Toss every few seconds.
  4. Place 1/3 of the rice in the bottom of another large pan. Cover with 1/2 of the chicken mixture. Continue layering remaining rice and chicken mixture. Cover and cook over very low heat until rice is tender, about 15 minutes. Transfer chicken-rice mixture to a large serving plate.
  5. Make garnish: Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add sliced onion; cook and stir until golden brown, about 5 minutes. Stir in raisins and soy sauce; cook for 1 minute. Scatter onion mixture over chicken-rice mixture. Top with hard-boiled eggs, lemon slices, and green onion.

curacha

Curacha dish is a traditional crab food popular in the province of Zamboanga in the Philippines. This dish features spanner crab, also known as red frog crab, which can only be found in the waters off the coast of Jolo, Sulu. Its name is a Chavacano phrase, which is a dialect spoken in the Zamboanga province. In Tausug, the same dish is known as Kagang Pamah.

The spanner crab is a hybrid crustacean. This crab's color is red and stays that way even when cooked. The most frequent method of preparing Curacha crab is steaming or boiling to preserve the tastes, although there are also other methods to make Curacha Curacha more appealing and delicious.One of such methods is 'adobo sa gata,' which is prepared with coconut milk, garlic, and ginger.

ginataang curacha recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 whole chicken
  2. 3 cups young coconut water
  3. 1 1/2 cups young coconut meat
  4. 2 cups chicken broth
  5. 2 1/2 tbsp fish sauce
  6. 1 small green papaya
  7. 1 cup hot pepper leaves
  8. 4 stalks lemon grass
  9. 1 medium onion
  10. 1 knob ginger
  11. 4 cloves garlic
  12. 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  13. 3 tbsp cooking oil

Instruction

  1. Heat a cooking pot and pour-in cooking oil.
  2. Saute garlic when the oil is hot.
  3. Put-in onions, ginger, and lemon grass, and then cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the chicken. Stir and cook until the color turns light brown (about 3 to 5 minutes)
  5. Add ground black pepper and fish sauce. Stir.
  6. Put-in the young coconut meat, young coconut water, and chicken broth. Allow to boil, and then simmer for 30 to 40 minutes.
  7. Add the green papaya. Cook for 5 minutes.
  8. Put-in the hot pepper leaves and cook for a minute more.
  9. That's it. Serve with steamed hot rice. Enjoy!

sinuglaw

Sinuglaw is a dish made up of grilled pig belly with ceviche of fish. This can be served as a main course or as an appetizer. The term "sinuglaw" comes from two popular cooking methods in the Visayas and Mindanao regions: sugba, which means to grill, and kinilaw, which means to cook by soaking in vinegar(tuba).

sinuglaw recipe

Ingredients

  1. 2 lb beef shank
  2. 2 stalks lemongrass
  3. 1 plum tomato
  4. 3 pcs long green chili (siling pansigang)
  5. 3 pcs hot red chili pepper(optional)
  6. 1 pc onion
  7. 7 cloves garlic
  8. 4 tbsp cooking oil
  9. 1 cup beef broth
  10. 2 20g pack Sinigang sa sampaloc mix
  11. 3 cups sliced unripe jackfruit
  12. 8 to 10 cups water
  13. 3 tbps fish sauce

Instruction

  1. Heat oil in a soup pot or pressure cooker.
  2. Put the garlic in once the oil gets hot. Cook until the garlic turns light brown.
  3. Add the beef shank. Cook one side for 30 seconds to one minute. Turn it over to cook the other side for about the same time.
  4. pour water. Let boil.
  5. Add the onion, tomato, and beef cube. Stir.
  6. Add the lemongrass. Cover the pressure cooker and cook for 30 minutes. Note: if using a regular soup pot, cook the beef in low heat for 2 to 3 hours. Add more water if needed.
  7. Safely remove the cover of the pressure cooker. Apply heat and bring the mixture to a boil.
  8. Stir-in the jackfruit and cook for 5 minutes.
  9. Add the peppers. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
  10. Pour the contents of the sinigang mix package. Stir. Add fish sauce. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  11. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
  12. Serve. Share and enjoy!

Mais binaki

Binaki, also known as steamed corn tamales, is made using milk, butter, sugar, and eggs. Binaki sweet corn is naturally sweet, aromatic, and fresh from the farm to the table.

Binaki is a popular dish in Cagayan De Oro and is also popular in neighboring provinces such as Bogo, Cebu, and Agusan del Sur, Misamis Oriental, and Northern Mindanao. Pintos is the Cebuano word for it. If you've ever visited the northern portion of Cebu, you'll understand. Pintos (Binaki) is another Bogo City corn dish that uses condensed milk, butter, or margarine to flavor the main ingredient of pulverized maize. The corn sheath is then stretched around it.

Mais binaki recipe

Ingredients

  1. 4 pcs sweet corn fresh with cob
  2. ¬º cup margarine
  3. ¬º cup powdered milk
  4. ‚Öì cup sugar
  5. ¬æ cup glutinous rice flour

Instruction

  1. Remove the corn husk and make sure you do not tear them because you will use them as a wrapping for the tamales. Set aside.
  2. Place the cob standing up and with a sharp knife, cut down the side to remove the kernels. Continue cutting while rotating the corn until all the kernels are removed. You can place the ear of corn inside a large bowl to keep all the kernels from spilling all over while cutting it.
  3. Using a food processor or blender, process the corn for 3-5 minutes or until smooth and creamy. Transfer into a large bowl and combine the margarine, powdered milk, sugar and glutinous rice flour. Mix until well blended.
  4. Use the husk and put a heaping tablespoon of corn mixture into the center. If the husk is too small, you may use 2-4 pcs and overlap them to each other. Fold husk lengthwise and bring two ends together.
  5. Place them into a steamer basket and steam for 20 minutes over boiling water with medium heat. Remove from the steamer and let it cool for 5 minutes. Serve and enjoy!

piaparan

Piaparan is a Maranao meal that consists of meat or seafood cooked in a coconut milk-based broth with grated coconut, garlic, onions, ginger, turmeric, young wild shallots, labuyo chile, and other vegetables and flavored with palapa.

Piaparan in native language means "shredded coconut" in Maranao and is one of the primary ingredients in today's recipe, Manuk na Piaparan.

chicken piaparan recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 whole chicken (preferably native)
  2. 1 pc large-size onion
  3. 2-4 pcs tomatoes
  4. 3 stack lemon grass
  5. 2 tumb ginger
  6. 2 tsp salt
  7. 1 tsp msg
  8. 2 cups water
  9. banana leaves for wrapping
  10. bamboo string for tying

Instruction

  1. Place the chicken in a pot. Add the onion, tomatoes, vetsin (optional) and salt. Pour in water just enough to cook the chicken. Boil for about 30 minutes to 1 hour depending on the chicken used. An older chicken usually takes longer to cook.
  2. In a separate bowl. Prepare the wrapper by lining the banana leaf on the bowl. Note: saba leaves affects the aroma and flavor of the tinuom compared to other banana leaves.
  3. Place the boiled chicken together with the rest of the ingredients into the bowl. Gather the ends of the banana leaves, making sure that the ingredients won‚Äôt drip by tying it with a string.
  4. Steam the wrapped ingredients and wait for a few minutes. (Another process is to place the wrapped ingredients again in a pot filled with water.)
  5. Remove the tinuom from heat. Serve and enjoy!
