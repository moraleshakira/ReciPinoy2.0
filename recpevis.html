la paz batchoy

"La Paz Batchoy" is a noodle soup that originated in Iloilo City's La Paz neighborhood. It has round noodles, crushed pork cracklings, beef loin, deep fried garlic, sliced spring onions, and pork broth. It's so aromatic that when your olfactory gland takes a whiff of it, you can't help but drool and mindlessly consume the dish in no time.
The flavor of the soup can be found at the crossroads of salty and sweet. Gulping the liquid component is comparable to drinking an inexpensive elixir in my opinion. Unlike Chinese noodles, Ilonggo's round noodle focuses on texture and fluffiness. The former is finer and more "al dente" than the latter. As previously said, La Paz Batchoy is regarded as one of the most famous Filipino noodle soups ever created.

la paz Batchoy recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 lb miki noodles
  2. 1 lb pork belly
  3. 1 lb pig's intestines(cleaned and boiled)
  4. 1/4 Ib pig liver
  5. 1 1/2 tsp salt
  6. 1/2 tsp gorund black pepper
  7. 2 tsp sugar
  8. 1 tsp shrimp paste, bagoong, or guinamus (optional)
  9. 1 tsp onion powder
  10. 1 cup pork cracklings or chicharon
  11. 3 tbsp spring onion
  12. 1/4 cup garlic (toasted)
  13. 7 cups water
  14. 1 boiled egg (optional toppings)

Instruction

  1. Let the water boil in a cooking pot
  2. Add in salt, onion powder, sugar, pepper and shrimp paste. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add the pork and let it cook until it is tender
  4. Add the liver and intestines and let it cook for another 10 minutes
  5. Remove the pork, intestines and liver from the caldo or broth. Set it aside.
  6. Slice the pork into thin strips
  7. Arrange the miki noodle in a serving bowl.
  8. Add on top of the noodles the liver, pork and intestines.
  9. Pour some broth and then garnish with toasted garlic,spring onions, and sliced boiled eggs.
  10. Share and enjoy!

Lumpiang Ubod

Lumpiang Ubod are fresh Filipino spring rolls with sautéed palm heart filling, handcrafted crepe wrappers, and a delicious garlic-peanut sauce. They're filling and tasty as a noon snack or main course.

Lumpiang ubod gets its name from the edible pith obtained from coconut trunks, ubod ("heart of palm"). The dish is named after the city of Silay in the province of Negros Occidental, on the island of Negros in the Visayas.

Lumpiang Ubod recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 lb hearts of palm
  2. 4 oz pork meat
  3. 12 pieces shrimp
  4. 2 tbsp parsley
  5. 1 1/2 tsp salt
  6. 1 pc onion
  7. 4 cloves garlic
  8. 3 tbsp cooking oil
  9. 3 tbsp Fish sauce
  10. 2 tsp ground black pepper
  11. 8 pieces lettuce leaves
  12. 1/2 cup roasted peanut (crushed)

    13. Fresh Lumpia Wrapper

  13. 1 cup all-purpose flour
  14. 2 pieces eggs
  15. 2 tablespoons canola oil
  16. 1 1/2 cups water
  17. 1/4 cup cooking oil or cooking oil spray

    18. Fresh Lumpia Wrapper

  18. 6 tablespoons brown sugar
  19. 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  20. 1 1/2 cups water
  21. 6 cloves garlic crushed
  22. 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Instruction

  1. Make the lumpia filling. Start by heating 3 tablespoons oil in a pan. Saute onion and garlic until the onion softens.
  2. Add pork and saute until light brown. Pour 1 ½ cup water into the pan. Let boil.
  3. Add condiments. Stir. Continue boiling in medium heat until the liquid evaporates.
  4. Add hearts of palm (ubod). Cook for 5 minutes. Toss every few seconds.
  5. Add carrots. Cook for 3 minutes.
  6. Put the shrimp into the pan. Toss and continue cooking until shrimp turns orange.
  7. Add brown sugar and stir until completely diluted.
  8. Put the garlic in the saucepan. Cook for 20 seconds.
  9. Combine cornstarch with 3 tablespoons water. Stir until blended. Pour the mixture into the pan. Continuously stir while cooking until mixture thickens. Set aside.
  10. Make the fresh lumpia wrapper by beating eggs in a bowl.
  11. Stir-in cooking oil and add water. Gradually add all-purpose flour. Continue stirring until all ingredients are well blended. Note: you can let the mixture pass through a strainer to filter lumps.
  12. Cook the wrapper by heating a non-stick pan. Grease it with oil. Pour wrapper mixture into the pan enough to cover the area. Continue cooking using medium heat until the wrapper can freely slide on the pan when you shake it. Transfer the wrapper to a clean plate. Note: do the same step until the mixture is fully consumed.
  13. Assemble the lumpiang ubod by laying a piece of wrapper flat on a plate. Place a piece of lettuce leaf over the wrapper. Scoop a good amount of filling (hearts of palm) over the lettuce. Gently wrap.
  14. Arrange the wrapped lumpia on a serving plate. Pour sauce over it and finish by topping it with crushed peanuts.
  15. Serve. Share and enjoy!

chicken binakol

Chicken Binakol is a delectable chicken soup. It is similar to Chicken Tinola, but the addition of coconut meat and coconut water distinguishes it.

Traditional techniques of cooking Chicken Binakol necessitate simmering native chicken for several hours. Native chicken meat is rougher than normal dressed chicken meat, yet it has greater flavor. Simmering local chicken for a longer period of time tenderizes the meat and releases all of the flavors.

chicken binakol recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 whole chicken
  2. 3 cups young coconut water
  3. 1 1/2 cups young coconut meat
  4. 2 cups chicken broth
  5. 2 1/2 tbsp fish sauce
  6. 1 small green papaya
  7. 1 cup hot pepper leaves
  8. 4 stalks lemon grass
  9. 1 medium onion
  10. 1 knob ginger
  11. 4 cloves garlic
  12. 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  13. 3 tbsp cooking oil

Instruction

  1. Heat a cooking pot and pour-in cooking oil.
  2. Saute garlic when the oil is hot.
  3. Put-in onions, ginger, and lemon grass, and then cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the chicken. Stir and cook until the color turns light brown (about 3 to 5 minutes)
  5. Add ground black pepper and fish sauce. Stir.
  6. Put-in the young coconut meat, young coconut water, and chicken broth. Allow to boil, and then simmer for 30 to 40 minutes.
  7. Add the green papaya. Cook for 5 minutes.
  8. Put-in the hot pepper leaves and cook for a minute more.
  9. That's it. Serve with steamed hot rice. Enjoy!

cansi

Cansi, also known as Kansi, is a substantial beef soup famed for its tangy and fresh flavor that originated in Bacolod and is a staple meal in the Ilocos area. This soup is typically made with beef shank broth, green langka(jackfruit), jalapeño peppers, and batuan(Garcinia binucao).

Many people claim that this Ilonggo delicacy is a cross between Bulalo and Sinigang. Similar to Bulalo, because of the use of beef shanks and bone marrows. Kansi, on the other hand, has the same mouthwatering sour and tangy qualities as Sinigang. The usage of green jackfruit as a vegetable or extender is the one distinguishing feature.

Cansi ilonggo recipe

Ingredients

  1. 2 lb beef shank
  2. 2 stalks lemongrass
  3. 1 plum tomato
  4. 3 pcs long green chili (siling pansigang)
  5. 3 pcs hot red chili pepper(optional)
  6. 1 pc onion
  7. 7 cloves garlic
  8. 4 tbsp cooking oil
  9. 1 cup beef broth
  10. 2 20g pack Sinigang sa sampaloc mix
  11. 3 cups sliced unripe jackfruit
  12. 8 to 10 cups water
  13. 3 tbps fish sauce

Instruction

  1. Heat oil in a soup pot or pressure cooker.
  2. Put the garlic in once the oil gets hot. Cook until the garlic turns light brown.
  3. Add the beef shank. Cook one side for 30 seconds to one minute. Turn it over to cook the other side for about the same time.
  4. pour water. Let boil.
  5. Add the onion, tomato, and beef cube. Stir.
  6. Add the lemongrass. Cover the pressure cooker and cook for 30 minutes. Note: if using a regular soup pot, cook the beef in low heat for 2 to 3 hours. Add more water if needed.
  7. Safely remove the cover of the pressure cooker. Apply heat and bring the mixture to a boil.
  8. Stir-in the jackfruit and cook for 5 minutes.
  9. Add the peppers. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
  10. Pour the contents of the sinigang mix package. Stir. Add fish sauce. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  11. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
  12. Serve. Share and enjoy!

inubarang manok

Inubarang Manok is a signature dish in Aklan, an island in the Visayas. Inubaran na Manok sa Gata is a chicken stewed in coconut milk with ubad or ubod as a souring agent and libas leaves as a flavoring element. Banana pith is also known as ubod. In contrast, ubad is an Ilonggo dialect for ubod.

Many people claim that this Ilonggo delicacy is a cross between Bulalo and Sinigang. Similar to Bulalo, because of the use of beef shanks and bone marrows. Kansi, on the other hand, has the same mouthwatering sour and tangy qualities as Sinigang. The usage of green jackfruit as a vegetable or extender is the one distinguishing feature.

inubaran na manok sa gata recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 whole chicken chopped (preferably “native” chicken)
  2. 2 cups Ubod or Ubad ng saging (pith of banana)
  3. Libas leaves (souring agent)
  4. 2 stalk Tanglad
  5. 1 1/2 tsp salt
  6. 2 cups Coconut milk
  7. 1 thumb Ginger
  8. 4 cloves Garlic
  9. 1 Onion
  10. 2 tsp ground black pepper

Instruction

  1. Slice ubad tube and sprinkle salt. Wash it with water, squeezed out juice and gently rub with palm to remove the fibers. Set aside
  2. Sauté the chicken in garlic, ginger and onion.
  3. Add chicken broth or water enough to cover the chicken until meat is soft.
  4. Halfway on the cooking process of the chicken add the ubad and the lemongrass.
  5. Three quarters of the way add the libas and half of the coconut milk.
  6. Once chicken is tender, add the remaining coconut milk.
  7. add salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve Hot and Enjoy with your favorite grilled food!.

suman moron

A chocolatey spin on sticky rice cake! Suman Moron is a Visayan delicacy made of glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and cocoa powder. This one is elevated by the addition of chopped peanuts.

Many people claim that this Ilonggo delicacy is a cross between Bulalo and Sinigang. Similar to Bulalo, because of the use of beef shanks and bone marrows. Kansi, on the other hand, has the same mouthwatering sour and tangy qualities as Sinigang. The usage of green jackfruit as a vegetable or extender is the one distinguishing feature.

suman moron recipe

Ingredients

    vanilla mixture

  1. 2 cup rice flour
  2. 2 cup glutinous rice flour
  3. 1 cup white sugar
  4. 2 tsp vanilla extract
  5. 3 cups coconut milk
  6. 2 cups Coconut milk

    7. chocolate mixture

  7. 2 cup rice flour
  8. 2 cup glutinous rice flour
  9. 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  10. 2 cup brown sugar
  11. 2 tsp vanilla extract
  12. 3 cups coconut milk
  13. 2 cup chopped roasted peanuts
  14. 12-14 pcs banana leaves(6x10 inch)

Instruction

    vanilla mixture

  1. In a deep pan, add rice flour, glutinous rice flour and sugar. Whisk to combine.
  2. Add coconut milk and vanilla extract.
  3. Simmer over low heat while stirring regularly until very thick, sticky dough forms. Transfer to a bowl and set aside.

    4. chocolate mixture

  4. In a deep pan, add rice flour, glutinous rice flour, brown sugar, and cocoa powder. Whisk to combine.
  5. Add coconut milk and vanilla extract.
  6. Simmer over low heat until it thickens slightly. Add chopped roasted peanuts and continue to simmer until very thick, sticky dough forms. Transfer to a bowl to cool down a bit.

    7. Assembly

  7. Heat cleaned banana leaves to make them pliable.
  8. Divide the dough into 12 -14 portions each. Take a portion of the white moron and place it on top of one side of the banana leaf and flatten it about 6x4- inch rectangle.
  9. Place a portion of chocolate moron and spread it on top but just within the white one.
  10. Gently roll the two into a cylinder then wrap it with the banana leaf. Tie the ends with a kitchen twine or use a thin strip of banana leaf as a twine. Do the same with the rest of the mixture.
  11. Place the rolled suman moron in a steamer basket and steam over low heat for 30 minutes.
  12. Take them out of the steamer and transfer to a plate to cool down. Serve with hot coffee or hot chocolate.

tinuom na manok

Tinuom is an Ilonggo dish that is popular in Cabatuan, Iloilo. It is a soup dish wrapped in banana leaves where its main ingredient is a native chicken, marinated with tomatoes, garlic, onions and lemon grass.

Tinu-om means "balot" in its original native language, hence Tinu-om na Manok means "binalot na manok." Many carinderias in Cabatuan attempted to make Tinu-om as well, but failed. The "timpla" could be one of the reasons they failed, according to one theory. "Timpla" refers to the taste proportionality of the combined substances. This means that there are no exact measurements for how many teaspoons of vetsin there are.

chicken tinuom recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 whole chicken (preferably native)
  2. 1 pc large-size onion
  3. 2-4 pcs tomatoes
  4. 3 stack lemon grass
  5. 2 tumb ginger
  6. 2 tsp salt
  7. 1 tsp msg
  8. 2 cups water
  9. banana leaves for wrapping
  10. bamboo string for tying

Instruction

  1. Place the chicken in a pot. Add the onion, tomatoes, vetsin (optional) and salt. Pour in water just enough to cook the chicken. Boil for about 30 minutes to 1 hour depending on the chicken used. An older chicken usually takes longer to cook.
  2. In a separate bowl. Prepare the wrapper by lining the banana leaf on the bowl. Note: saba leaves affects the aroma and flavor of the tinuom compared to other banana leaves.
  3. Place the boiled chicken together with the rest of the ingredients into the bowl. Gather the ends of the banana leaves, making sure that the ingredients won’t drip by tying it with a string.
  4. Steam the wrapped ingredients and wait for a few minutes. (Another process is to place the wrapped ingredients again in a pot filled with water.)
  5. Remove the tinuom from heat. Serve and enjoy!

tuslob buwa

Tuslob buwa is a popular Filipino street meal found in Cebu City's Pasil area. Its main ingredients are sautéed hog liver and brain with garlic, onions, shrimp paste, and chilis.

TUSLOB BUWA - this has to be the most unusual food Cebu has to offer. It is a very popular meal in Cebu, an exotic delicacy with a combination of spices, fragrant aromas, and, of course, pig's brain as the main ingredient. When there are pig brains, it seems strange, but once you try it, you will understand. It is tasty and goes well with "Puso" (rice wrapped and boiled in a triangle casing made of braided coconut leaves), also known as Hanging Rice.

tuslob buwa recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1/8 kilo pig's brain
  2. 1/8 kilo pig's liver/ liver spread(blended)
  3. 1 clove garlic
  4. 1 onion
  5. 2 tbsp salted shrimp paste
  6. 5 tbsp water
  7. 3 tbsp butter or oil
  8. 2 cups water
  9. Diluted cornstarch in water

Instruction

  1. saute garlic and onion in hot oil
  2. add pig's brain and the blended pork liver, stir continuously.
  3. add teh salted shrimp paste, season with salt and pepper.
  4. add water, until the consistency is thick and will bubbles(buwa).
  5. add the diluted cornstarch mixture then simmer.
  6. turn off the heat. Ready to dip (tuslob) the bubbly(buwa) pig's brain with puso(hanging rice) enjoy!
