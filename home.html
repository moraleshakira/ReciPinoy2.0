Best of Luzon

A traditional Ilocano-Filipino cuisine from the Ilocos area, "Pakbet," derived from the phrase "Pinakebbet," has gained popularity throughout the country. It consist a variety of vegetables sautéed in fish paste.

Best of Visayas

The ultimate Filipino noodle soup is a favorite for a reason. Dig into firm egg noodles that swim in a slow-cooked broth with lots of flavorful toppings: crunchy garlic, chicharon bits, green onions, and lots of pork!

Best of Mindanao

Pastil, or pastel, is a Filipino packed rice dish made with steamed rice wrapped in banana leaves with dry shredded beef, chicken, or fish. It originates from the Maguindanao people and is a popular, cheap breakfast meal in Mindanao, especially among Muslim Filipinos.

Filipino Breakfast

Sinangag, or garlic fried rice, is the foundation of most Filipino breakfasts. Sinangag, a fragrant dish loaded with crisp fried garlic, is a basic dish with only a few ingredients - garlic, salt, pepper, oil, and rice - but it requires a lot of effort and accuracy to guarantee it's prepared properly.

Filipino Merienda

Merienda, which mostly refers to a light afternoon snack that was passed down to the Filipino people during Spain's colonization of the country. In Filipino cuisine, merienda is usually eaten at around 3 or 4 p.m. It's a small snack that can be savory or sweet and will keep you going till dinner.

Filipino Desserts

Desserts are quite popular among Filipinos. It simply seems like a regular meal would be incomplete without a dessert to round it out, you know? Whether it's something sweet, something sour, something hot, something cold, or both!
